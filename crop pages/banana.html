Banana Crop Manual

Introduction

Banana is one of the most important fruit crops grown in tropical and subtropical regions. It is cultivated for its nutritious fruits that are rich in carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals. Bananas are used for direct consumption, processing, and medicinal purposes.

Soil Requirements

Bananas grow best in deep, rich, well-drained loamy soils with high organic matter. The crop prefers soils with good water-holding capacity and neutral pH (6.0–7.5).

  • Soil Type: Loamy, alluvial soils
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.5
  • Organic Matter: High content recommended

Water Requirements

Banana is a water-loving plant and requires abundant moisture for good growth and fruit development. Regular irrigation is essential, especially during dry periods.

  • Water Requirement: 1200–2200 mm per year
  • Irrigation Method: Drip or basin irrigation
  • Water Level: Soil should be kept consistently moist, avoid waterlogging

Growth Duration

Banana is a medium to long-duration crop. Depending on the variety and growing conditions, it takes:

Variety Duration
Dwarf varieties 10–12 months
Medium varieties 12–14 months
Tall varieties 14–16 months

Timely irrigation, proper nutrition, and pest control are important to achieve optimum yield.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Banana is a heavy feeder and requires adequate nutrition throughout its growth cycle. Both organic manure and chemical fertilizers play a vital role in productivity:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 200–250 kg/ha
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 60–70 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 200–300 kg/ha
Organic Manure 10–15 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Banana plants are prone to several pests and diseases. Proper management ensures healthy plants and good yields:

  • Common pests: Banana weevil, Aphids, Thrips
  • Diseases: Panama wilt, Bunchy top, Sigatoka leaf spot
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), crop rotation, resistant varieties, and proper field sanitation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Carbofuran, Mancozeb, Copper oxychloride

Harvesting

Bananas are harvested when the fruits are mature but still green. Harvesting time depends on the variety and growing conditions. Proper handling after harvest ensures better shelf life and market value.

  • Harvesting Time: 11–15 months after planting depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Dehanding, grading, cleaning, and storage at cool temperatures
