Corn Crop Manual

Introduction

Corn, also called maize, is a major cereal crop grown worldwide for food, feed, and industrial purposes. It thrives in warm climates with fertile soils and adequate rainfall. Proper management of soil, irrigation, nutrients, and pests ensures high yield.

Soil Requirements

Corn grows best in well-drained, fertile loamy soils with good organic matter content. Neutral to slightly acidic soils (pH 5.8–7.0) promote healthy growth.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, sandy loam
  • Soil pH: 5.8–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 2–4% recommended

Water Requirements

Corn requires regular watering, especially during germination, tasseling, and grain filling stages. Both rainfed and irrigated conditions can be used depending on climate.

  • Water Requirement: 500–800 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Furrow or sprinkler irrigation
  • Water Level: Maintain moist soil during critical growth stages

Growth Duration

The duration of corn growth depends on the variety and climatic conditions:

Variety Duration
Short-duration 90–100 days
Medium-duration 100–120 days
Long-duration 120–150 days

Timely field monitoring and management practices help achieve optimal yield.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization is essential for corn growth and productivity. Organic manure improves soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers supply necessary nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 120–150 kg/ha (split doses)
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 60–80 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 60–80 kg/ha
Organic Manure 5–10 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Corn is vulnerable to various pests and diseases. Proper management is essential for high yield:

  • Common pests: Stem borer, Fall armyworm, Aphids
  • Diseases: Leaf blight, Rust, Smut
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), resistant varieties, crop rotation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Chlorpyrifos, Emamectin benzoate

Harvesting

Corn is harvested when the grains are fully mature and dry. Proper harvesting ensures maximum grain yield and quality. Post-harvest handling includes drying, storage, and transportation.

  • Harvesting Time: 90–150 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Drying, threshing, and storage in dry conditions
