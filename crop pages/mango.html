Mango Crop Manual

Introduction

Mango is a tropical fruit crop widely grown for its sweet and nutritious fruits. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soils. Proper care including irrigation, pruning, fertilization, and pest control ensures good fruit yield and quality.

Soil Requirements

Mango grows best in deep, fertile, well-drained soils. Sandy loam or alluvial soils with good organic content support healthy growth. Slightly acidic to neutral soil pH (5.5–7.5) is ideal.

  • Soil Type: Sandy loam, alluvial
  • Soil pH: 5.5–7.5
  • Organic Matter: 1–3% recommended

Water Requirements

Mango requires moderate irrigation. Young trees need regular watering, while mature trees are more drought-tolerant. Overwatering must be avoided to prevent root rot.

  • Water Requirement: 600–800 mm per year
  • Irrigation Method: Drip or basin irrigation
  • Water Level: Maintain moist soil, avoid waterlogging

Growth Duration

Mango trees take a few years to reach full fruit-bearing capacity:

Variety Time to Fruit
Dwarf/Hybrid 2–3 years
Regular/Standard 4–6 years

Regular monitoring and pruning help maintain healthy growth and encourage fruit production.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Proper nutrition is crucial for mango tree health and fruit quality. Organic manure improves soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers provide essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 200–250 g per tree per year
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 50–60 g per tree per year
Potassium (K₂O) 150–200 g per tree per year
Organic Manure 20–30 kg per tree per year

Pest and Disease Management

Mango trees are vulnerable to pests and diseases. Timely management is important to reduce crop loss:

  • Common pests: Mango hoppers, Mealybugs, Fruit flies
  • Diseases: Anthracnose, Powdery mildew, Bacterial black spot
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), resistant varieties, pruning, and proper sanitation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Imidacloprid, Carbendazim

Harvesting

Mangoes are harvested when the fruits reach full color and sugar content. Proper harvesting and post-harvest handling ensure longer shelf life and better market quality.

  • Harvesting Time: 3–6 months after flowering depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Grading, cleaning, and storage under cool conditions
