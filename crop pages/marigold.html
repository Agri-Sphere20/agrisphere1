Marigold Crop Manual

Introduction

Marigold is a popular ornamental flower cultivated for its bright, vibrant blooms. It grows best in warm climates with well-drained soils. Marigolds are used for decoration, garlands, and even pest-repellent companion planting. Proper care ensures continuous flowering and high-quality blooms.

Soil Requirements

Marigolds thrive in well-drained sandy loam soils rich in organic matter. Neutral to slightly acidic soils (pH 6.0–7.0) favor healthy growth and abundant flowering.

  • Soil Type: Sandy loam, loamy soil
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 1–3% recommended

Water Requirements

Marigold requires moderate watering. Watering should be regular but avoid waterlogging, which can cause root rot and fungal diseases.

  • Water Requirement: 300–500 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Furrow or drip irrigation
  • Water Level: Keep soil moist, avoid excessive water

Growth Duration

Marigold is a short-duration flowering crop, depending on the variety:

Variety Duration
Short-duration 60–70 days
Medium-duration 70–90 days
Long-duration 90–120 days

Regular monitoring and deadheading encourage continuous blooming and maintain plant health.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization promotes healthy growth and bright flowers. Organic compost improves soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers supply essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 50–70 kg/ha
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 30–40 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 40–60 kg/ha
Organic Manure 3–5 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Marigolds are generally hardy but may be affected by some pests and diseases. Proper care ensures healthy plants and abundant flowers:

  • Common pests: Aphids, Leaf miners, Thrips
  • Diseases: Powdery mildew, Alternaria leaf spot
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), proper spacing, resistant varieties, timely removal of infected leaves
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Imidacloprid, Mancozeb

Harvesting

Marigold flowers are harvested when fully open for garlands, decoration, or commercial use. Frequent harvesting promotes continuous flowering.

  • Harvesting Time: 60–120 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Sorting, cleaning, and storing in cool dry conditions
