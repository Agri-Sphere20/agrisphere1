Okra Crop Manual

Introduction

Ladiesfinger, also known as okra or bhindi, is a widely cultivated vegetable crop valued for its edible green pods. It grows best in warm climates and fertile, well-drained soil. Proper irrigation, fertilization, and pest management ensure good yield and pod quality.

Soil Requirements

Ladiesfinger thrives in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Neutral to slightly acidic soil (pH 6.0–7.0) favors healthy growth and high pod production.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, sandy loam
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 1–3% recommended

Water Requirements

Ladiesfinger requires regular watering, especially during flowering and pod formation. Avoid waterlogging to prevent root diseases.

  • Water Requirement: 400–600 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Drip or furrow irrigation
  • Water Level: Maintain moist soil, avoid overwatering

Growth Duration

Ladiesfinger is a short-duration crop that can be harvested multiple times during the season:

Variety Duration
Short-duration 50–60 days
Medium-duration 60–75 days
Long-duration 75–90 days

Regular harvesting of pods encourages continuous production and maintains plant health.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization is important for healthy growth and high pod yield. Organic manure enhances soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers supply necessary nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 50–70 kg/ha
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 30–40 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 30–50 kg/ha
Organic Manure 3–5 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Ladiesfinger is susceptible to pests and diseases. Proper care ensures good yield and quality:

  • Common pests: Aphids, Whiteflies, Shoot and Fruit borers
  • Diseases: Powdery mildew, Leaf spot, Root rot
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), resistant varieties, proper spacing, and timely harvesting
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Imidacloprid, Mancozeb

Harvesting

Ladiesfinger pods are harvested when tender and green. Frequent harvesting encourages more pod production and maintains quality. Handle pods carefully to avoid damage.

  • Harvesting Time: 50–90 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Grading, cleaning, and storage in cool, dry conditions
