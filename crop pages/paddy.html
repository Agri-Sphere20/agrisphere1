Paddy Crop Manual

Introduction

Paddy, also known as rice, is one of the most important staple crops worldwide. It is cultivated primarily in regions with abundant water and warm climates. Paddy cultivation involves careful management of soil, water, fertilizers, and pest control to ensure a high yield.

Soil Requirements

Paddy grows best in soils that can retain water effectively. Clayey or loamy soils rich in organic matter are ideal. Slightly acidic to neutral soil pH (5.5–7.0) promotes healthy growth.

  • Soil Type: Clayey, loamy
  • Soil pH: 5.5–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 2–4% recommended

Water Requirements

Paddy is a water-intensive crop. Maintaining standing water in fields helps control weeds and provides optimal growth conditions. Flood irrigation or controlled water release is used depending on the region.

  • Water Requirement: 1200–1500 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Flooding or controlled irrigation
  • Water Level: 5–10 cm depth in vegetative stage

Growth Duration

The time taken from transplanting to harvesting varies according to the variety:

Variety Duration
Short-duration 100–120 days
Medium-duration 120–140 days
Long-duration 150+ days

Regular monitoring during each stage is necessary for achieving maximum yield.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilizer application is crucial for optimal growth. Organic manure enhances soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers provide essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 100–120 kg/ha (split doses)
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 40–60 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 40–60 kg/ha
Organic Manure 5–10 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Paddy is susceptible to several pests and diseases. Proper management prevents yield loss:

  • Common pests: Stem borer, Leaf folder, Brown planthopper
  • Diseases: Blast, Sheath blight, Bacterial leaf blight
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), crop rotation, use of resistant varieties
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Chlorantraniliprole, Imidacloprid

Harvesting

Paddy is usually harvested when the grains are mature and golden-yellow in color. Proper timing ensures minimal loss. After harvesting, drying and storage are essential to maintain grain quality.

  • Harvesting Time: When grains reach full maturity
  • Post-harvest Handling: Sun-drying, storage in dry conditions
