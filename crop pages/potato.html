Potato Crop Manual

Introduction

Potato is a widely cultivated tuber crop valued for its edible underground tubers. It grows best in cool climates with well-drained, fertile soil. Proper care including irrigation, fertilization, and pest management ensures high yield and good tuber quality.

Soil Requirements

Potato grows well in light, well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Slightly acidic soils (pH 5.5–6.5) are ideal for tuber development.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, sandy loam
  • Soil pH: 5.5–6.5
  • Organic Matter: 2–4% recommended

Water Requirements

Potato requires regular irrigation, especially during tuber initiation and growth. Overwatering must be avoided to prevent rotting and fungal diseases.

  • Water Requirement: 500–700 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Furrow or drip irrigation
  • Water Level: Maintain moist soil, avoid waterlogging

Growth Duration

The duration of potato growth depends on the variety:

Variety Duration
Early 75–90 days
Medium 90–120 days
Late 120–150 days

Timely field monitoring and proper care are necessary to achieve optimal yield.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization is essential for high yield and tuber quality. Organic manure improves soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers supply essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 120–150 kg/ha (split doses)
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 50–60 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 100–150 kg/ha
Organic Manure 10–15 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Potato plants are susceptible to pests and diseases. Proper management ensures healthy plants and good tuber production:

  • Common pests: Potato tuber moth, Aphids, Colorado potato beetle
  • Diseases: Late blight, Early blight, Bacterial wilt
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), crop rotation, resistant varieties, proper irrigation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Mancozeb, Imidacloprid

Harvesting

Potatoes are harvested when the plants have matured and the tuber skins have hardened. Proper harvesting and post-harvest handling ensure minimal damage and longer storage life.

  • Harvesting Time: 75–150 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Curing, cleaning, and storage in cool, dry conditions
