Sugarcane Crop Manual

Introduction

Sugarcane is a tropical and subtropical crop cultivated mainly for sugar production. It grows in regions with warm temperatures, good rainfall, and fertile soils. Successful sugarcane cultivation depends on proper soil preparation, irrigation, fertilization, and pest control.

Soil Requirements

Sugarcane prefers deep, well-drained loamy soils with high organic content. Slightly acidic to neutral soil pH (6.0–7.5) supports optimum growth.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, alluvial
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.5
  • Organic Matter: 2–5% recommended

Water Requirements

Sugarcane is a water-intensive crop that requires consistent moisture. Adequate irrigation ensures better cane growth and higher sugar content. Drip or furrow irrigation is commonly used depending on water availability.

  • Water Requirement: 1500–2500 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Drip, furrow, or flood irrigation
  • Water Level: Maintain moist soil, avoid waterlogging

Growth Duration

Sugarcane requires a longer growing period depending on the variety:

Variety Duration
Early-maturing 10–12 months
Medium-maturing 12–14 months
Late-maturing 15–18 months

Regular field monitoring ensures proper growth, sugar accumulation, and timely management practices.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization enhances cane growth and sugar content. Organic manure improves soil health, while chemical fertilizers supply essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 100–150 kg/ha (split doses)
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 50–70 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 60–80 kg/ha
Organic Manure 10–15 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Sugarcane is prone to pests and diseases that can reduce yield. Proper management ensures healthy crops:

  • Common pests: Sugarcane borer, White grub, Aphids
  • Diseases: Red rot, Smut, Leaf scald
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), resistant varieties, crop rotation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Chlorpyrifos, Imidacloprid

Harvesting

Sugarcane is harvested when the stalks are mature and high in sugar content. Proper timing ensures maximum juice yield. Post-harvest handling includes cleaning, transportation, and processing.

  • Harvesting Time: 10–18 months depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Transport to mills, remove extraneous matter
