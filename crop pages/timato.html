Tomato Crop Manual

Introduction

Tomato is a widely grown vegetable crop valued for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained fertile soils. Proper care including irrigation, fertilization, staking, and pest management ensures healthy growth and high yield.

Soil Requirements

Tomato grows best in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Slightly acidic to neutral soils (pH 6.0–7.0) promote good fruit set and quality.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, sandy loam
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 2–3% recommended

Water Requirements

Tomato requires regular watering, especially during flowering and fruiting. Avoid waterlogging to prevent root rot and fungal diseases.

  • Water Requirement: 600–800 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Drip or furrow irrigation
  • Water Level: Keep soil consistently moist, avoid overwatering

Growth Duration

Tomato is a short-to-medium duration crop depending on the variety:

Variety Duration
Early 70–90 days
Medium 90–120 days
Late 120–150 days

Regular monitoring and staking improve fruit quality and yield.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization is essential for healthy plants and high-quality fruit. Organic manure improves soil fertility, while chemical fertilizers supply essential nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 100–150 kg/ha (split doses)
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 50–70 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 100–150 kg/ha
Organic Manure 5–10 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Tomato is susceptible to pests and diseases. Proper care ensures healthy plants and good fruit yield:

  • Common pests: Aphids, Whiteflies, Tomato fruit borer
  • Diseases: Early blight, Late blight, Fusarium wilt
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), crop rotation, resistant varieties, proper irrigation
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Imidacloprid, Mancozeb

Harvesting

Tomato fruits are harvested when fully colored and firm. Timely harvesting ensures good market quality and continuous fruit production.

  • Harvesting Time: 70–150 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Sorting, grading, and storage in cool conditions
