Urad Dal Crop Manual

Introduction

Urad Dal, also known as black gram, is a legume crop widely grown in India and other tropical regions. It is valued for its protein-rich seeds and nitrogen-fixing ability. Proper care in terms of soil, irrigation, fertilization, and pest control ensures a good yield.

Soil Requirements

Urad dal grows best in well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils with good organic matter content. Neutral to slightly acidic soils (pH 6.0–7.0) are ideal for growth and pod formation.

  • Soil Type: Loamy, sandy loam
  • Soil pH: 6.0–7.0
  • Organic Matter: 1–3% recommended

Water Requirements

Urad dal requires moderate water, especially during flowering and pod development. Avoid waterlogging, as it may damage the roots.

  • Water Requirement: 400–600 mm per growing season
  • Irrigation Method: Furrow or drip irrigation
  • Water Level: Keep soil moist, avoid excess water

Growth Duration

The duration of urad dal growth depends on the variety and climate:

Variety Duration
Short-duration 90–100 days
Medium-duration 100–120 days
Long-duration 120–150 days

Timely monitoring and proper care improve pod yield and quality.

Fertilizers and Nutrient Management

Balanced fertilization is important for good growth and seed production. Organic manure improves soil fertility, while fertilizers provide necessary nutrients:

Nutrient Recommended Quantity
Nitrogen (N) 15–20 kg/ha
Phosphorus (P₂O₅) 30–40 kg/ha
Potassium (K₂O) 20–30 kg/ha
Organic Manure 3–5 tons/ha

Pest and Disease Management

Urad dal is susceptible to several pests and diseases. Proper management ensures high yield and seed quality:

  • Common pests: Aphids, Thrips, Pod borers
  • Diseases: Powdery mildew, Cercospora leaf spot, Root rot
  • Control methods: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), resistant varieties, crop rotation, and timely harvesting
  • Recommended pesticides (use as per guidelines): Imidacloprid, Mancozeb

Harvesting

Urad dal is harvested when pods mature and seeds are fully developed. Proper harvesting and post-harvest handling ensure seed quality and storage longevity.

  • Harvesting Time: 90–150 days depending on variety
  • Post-harvest Handling: Threshing, cleaning, and storage in dry conditions
