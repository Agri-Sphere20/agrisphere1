Anthracnose Disease

Introduction

Anthracnose is a fungal disease affecting many crops such as beans, cucurbits, mango, grapes, and chillies. It is caused by several species of Colletotrichum. The disease leads to dark sunken lesions on leaves, stems, fruits, and pods, causing major yield and quality losses.

Causes

Anthracnose is mainly caused by fungi belonging to the Colletotrichum genus. Warm, humid, and rainy conditions promote the rapid spread of the disease. Spores can survive in infected crop residues and spread through rain splash, wind, or contaminated tools.

Symptoms

  • Dark, sunken spots with concentric rings on fruits
  • Irregular brown or black spots on leaves
  • Stem cankers that weaken plant growth
  • Premature fruit drop and shriveling
  • Reduced seed germination in infected pods

Prevention

  • Use disease-free and certified seeds
  • Practice crop rotation with non-host crops
  • Ensure proper spacing and air circulation
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris
  • Apply protective fungicide sprays during wet seasons

Treatment

  • Apply fungicides such as mancozeb, carbendazim, or copper-based formulations
  • Spray bio-control agents like Trichoderma in the soil
  • Prune and destroy heavily infected plant parts

Fertilizers and Nutrition

Balanced fertilization reduces anthracnose severity. Excess nitrogen favors disease spread, while potassium and micronutrients strengthen plant resistance.

Nutrient Purpose Application Tips
Nitrogen (N) Promotes growth but excess increases disease Apply in controlled amounts
Phosphorus (P) Enhances root strength and plant vigor Apply at planting stage
Potassium (K) Boosts immunity and reduces fungal spread Apply throughout crop cycle
Micronutrients (Zn, B, Mn) Improve plant resistance to infection Apply as foliar sprays when deficiency appears

Additional Tips

  • Inspect crops regularly, especially during rainy seasons
  • Use resistant varieties wherever available
  • Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness
  • Follow integrated pest and disease management practices
