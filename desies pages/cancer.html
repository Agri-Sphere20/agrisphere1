Canker Disease

Introduction

Canker disease is a serious condition affecting the stems, branches, and fruits of many plants, including citrus, apples, and tomatoes. It is caused by fungi and bacteria, leading to sunken lesions and cracking of bark. Severe infections can cause dieback, fruit drop, and even plant death.

Causes

Canker disease is mainly caused by fungal pathogens like Phomopsis and bacterial pathogens like Xanthomonas or Pseudomonas. Wounds, pruning cuts, insect damage, and environmental stress allow pathogens to infect the plant.

Symptoms

  • Sunken, dark lesions on stems, branches, and fruits
  • Cracked bark with oozing gum or resin
  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves near affected areas
  • Dieback of shoots and branches
  • Premature fruit drop with blemishes

Prevention

  • Use resistant plant varieties where available
  • Practice crop rotation and proper orchard hygiene
  • Disinfect pruning tools before use
  • Avoid injuries to plants during cultivation
  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts

Treatment

  • Apply copper-based fungicides or bactericides to infected areas
  • Prune and remove infected branches well below the affected part
  • Apply wound dressing or tree paint after pruning to prevent reinfection
  • Improve plant vigor through proper irrigation and fertilization

Fertilizers and Nutrition

Balanced nutrition strengthens plants against canker infection. Adequate potassium and calcium improve resistance, while excessive nitrogen can make plants more vulnerable.

Nutrient Purpose Application Tips
Nitrogen (N) Supports growth but excess increases disease risk Apply in moderate amounts
Phosphorus (P) Encourages strong root development Use during early growth stages
Potassium (K) Improves immunity and wound healing Apply regularly for optimum health
Calcium (Ca) Strengthens cell walls and reduces cracking Apply gypsum or foliar calcium sprays

Additional Tips

  • Inspect orchards regularly for early signs of infection
  • Maintain proper tree spacing to improve airflow
  • Ensure good drainage to avoid waterlogging stress
  • Implement integrated pest and disease management practices
