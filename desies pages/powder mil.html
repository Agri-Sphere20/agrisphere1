Powdery Mildew

Introduction

Powdery mildew is a common fungal disease that affects a wide range of plants, including vegetables, fruits, and ornamentals. It appears as white, powdery spots on the leaves, stems, and sometimes flowers and fruits. If untreated, it weakens plants, reduces photosynthesis, and lowers yield and quality.

Causes

The disease is caused by various fungi in the order Erysiphales. Powdery mildew spreads rapidly in warm, dry conditions with high humidity at night. Overcrowding of plants, lack of air circulation, and susceptible plant varieties favor its development.

Symptoms

  • White, powdery fungal growth on leaves, stems, or buds
  • Yellowing and distortion of leaves
  • Reduced flowering and fruit set
  • Premature leaf drop and poor plant vigor

Prevention

  • Plant resistant varieties whenever available
  • Provide proper spacing and good air circulation
  • Avoid excess nitrogen fertilization
  • Remove and destroy infected plant material
  • Apply preventive fungicidal sprays in conditions favorable to the disease

Treatment

  • Use fungicides such as sulfur, potassium bicarbonate, or systemic fungicides like triazoles
  • Prune heavily infected leaves to slow spread
  • Improve air circulation and reduce humidity

Fertilizers and Nutrition

Balanced nutrition strengthens plants against powdery mildew. Phosphorus and potassium improve resistance, while excess nitrogen can increase susceptibility.

Nutrient Purpose Application Tips
Nitrogen (N) Encourages leaf growth Apply moderately; excess promotes disease
Phosphorus (P) Supports root growth and plant resilience Use based on soil test recommendations
Potassium (K) Improves tissue strength and disease tolerance Apply regularly to maintain balance
Micronutrients (Zn, Mn, Mg) Boost overall plant immunity Use foliar sprays or soil amendments

Additional Tips

  • Inspect plants regularly, especially during dry, warm days
  • Rotate crops to reduce fungal buildup in soil
  • Disinfect tools to prevent spread
  • Maintain good garden hygiene to reduce infection risk
